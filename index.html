By Pastor Bryan Threlkeld

Pastor Bryan Threlkeld is dedicated to guiding individuals on their spiritual journeys. Through his sermons and teachings, he inspires a deeper connection with faith and community.

    How to be Accepted by God

    There is nothing more crucial than understanding how we enter into a relationship with God and how we grow closer to Him and become spiritually mature. Unfortunately, misconceptions abound in both of these areas. Join us as Pastor Bryan launches into a new series, exploring what we call 'Free Grace Theology.'

    Assurance

    People around the world live in fear of what will happen after they die. Unfortunately, many Christians also fear what will happen when they die and it keeps them stuck in their relationship with God. Join us as we look at how you can be ABSOLUTELY sure, so you don't need to live in fear anymore.

    Is Grace Risky? Yes.

    So all this talk about salvation being by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone... it sounds too easy, right? I mean, doesn't that mean people could take advantage of God's grace? Have you ever wondered: "Can a person put their faith in Jesus, get their 'fire insurance,' and then live however they feel like on this earth, and still go to heaven?" Join us as Pastor Bryan addresses the question: "Is Grace Risky?" The answer may surprise you!

    Pastor Bryan

    Is Grace Risky? No.

    We've been talking about how gracious our God is, and how salvation is an absolutely free gift received by faith in Jesus alone. But now it's time to turn our attention to another one of God's attributes... His justice. Join us as Pastor Bryan breaks down a passage in Galatians 6, and introduces us to a principle as certain as the law of gravity... the law of sowing and reaping.

    When God Says "Enough"

    The Bible speaks of God as loving, gracious, kind, patient and slow to anger. But there are times when people (believers and unbelievers) push the envelope, and God says: 'Enough is enough.' Join us as Pastor Bryan explores three surprising stories in the New Testament!

    Judge Not

    Whether you are rich or poor, educated or uneducated, a believer or an unbeliever... you can fall into this sin. Politicians, prostitutes and pastors are all guilty of it at times. And it's somewhat insidious, because it's the one sin for which we tend to make excuses. Join us as Pastor Bryan concludes our in-depth study on the subject of God's grace.
